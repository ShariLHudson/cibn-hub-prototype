=== INVITING UP TO 1,000 LINKEDIN CONNECTIONS TO AN EVENT ===

Purpose
The CIBN Chrome Extension allows members to invite large numbers of LinkedIn connections to events quickly. This tool helps increase event attendance and visibility by sending invitations to many first-degree connections efficiently.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1 — Create or Open Your LinkedIn Event
Go to LinkedIn, click My Network, then click Events. Create a new event or open an existing event you want to promote.

Step 2 — Complete Event Details
Ensure your event includes the title, description, date, time, and location or registration link.

Step 3 — Mark Yourself as Attending
Click Attend so you are marked as attending your own event.

Step 4 — Open the Invite Window
Click the Invite or Share option on your event page.

Step 5 — Launch the CIBN Chrome Extension
Open the CIBN Chrome Extension from the top right of your browser.

Step 6 — Start Loading Your Connections
Use the extension’s auto-scroll or auto-load feature to begin loading your LinkedIn connections into the invitation window.

Step 7 — Allow the System to Populate Connections
The tool will scroll through your LinkedIn connection list and load potential invitees automatically.

Step 8 — Monitor the Number of Loaded Connections
Watch the number of connections being loaded into the invitation window.

Step 9 — Stop at Your Desired Number
Click Stop once the number of connections reaches your desired level (up to approximately 1,000 invitations depending on LinkedIn limits).

Step 10 — Send Invitations
Click Invite to send the invitations to your selected LinkedIn connections.

Important Notes

• Invitations are sent to your first-degree LinkedIn connections.  
• LinkedIn may apply weekly limits on event invitations.  
• Larger networks allow more invitations to be sent.  
• Inviting people who are relevant to the event topic improves attendance.

Result
Using the extension allows members to invite large numbers of connections quickly, helping fill networking events, webinars, and meetings more efficiently.

=== FOLLOWING UP WITH YOUR LINKEDIN EVENT INVITEES ===
Purpose
Following up with people who were invited to your LinkedIn event helps convert “Interested” responses into actual registrations and attendance. Many people click interested but do not complete the registration process unless they receive a follow-up message.
Step-by-Step Instructions
Step 1 — Wait After Sending Invitations
After sending LinkedIn event invitations, allow approximately 24 hours for people to respond.
Step 2 — Open Your LinkedIn Event
Go to LinkedIn and open the event you created.

Step 3 — View the Networking Section
Inside the event page, click the Networking or Attendees section to see people who marked themselves as “Interested” or “Going”.
Step 4 — Identify First-Degree Connections
Look for people who are your first-degree connections. These are the people you can message directly.
Step 5 — Send a Follow-Up Message
Send a short LinkedIn message to the person thanking them for their interest in the event.
Step 6 — Share the Registration Link
If your event requires registration, include the registration link in the message.
Step 7 — Clarify Event Details
Briefly remind them of the event topic, date, and why the event may be valuable for them.
Step 8 — Invite Questions
Let them know they can ask questions if they want more information about the event.
Step 9 — Repeat for Additional Interested Attendees
Continue messaging other attendees who clicked “Interested” or “Going”.
Step 10 — Track Responses
Pay attention to who registers or responds so you can focus on engaged participants.
Result
Following up with invitees significantly increases registration rates and event attendance by turning casual interest into confirmed participation.

=== NETWORKING SUCCESS PATH — STAGE 1: BUILD ===
Purpose
Stage 1 focuses on building a strong foundation of professional relationships. Members begin expanding their network and identifying potential collaborating partners.
Step-by-Step Instructions
Step 1 — Create New Professional Connections
Begin building your network by connecting with professionals in your industry or community.
Step 2 — Aim for Your First 25 Connections
Focus on forming meaningful relationships with at least 25 professionals.
Step 3 — Identify Potential Collaborating Partners
Look for professionals who serve the same audience but offer different services.
Step 4 — Schedule One-on-One Conversations
Meet with people individually to learn about their business and explore collaboration opportunities.
Step 5 — Begin Building Trust
Focus on learning about others, understanding their businesses, and offering support where appropriate.
Result
At this stage, the goal is to create a strong relationship foundation and identify potential collaborators.
=== NETWORKING SUCCESS PATH — STAGE 2: SOCIALIZE ===

Step-by-Step Instructions
Step 1 — Continue Expanding Your Network
Grow your list of professional connections through networking events and online platforms.
Step 2 — Strengthen Existing Relationships
