1. ASSESS COMMITMENT 
Are you looking for that “big” networking group? Why? Do you realize that a bona fide
networking expert can only meet 20 people an hour? So what is the point at being at an
event with 200 people? This type of networking is like looking for a one-night stand. It is not
based on a commitment or a long-term relationship, so it ends with emptiness. 
The reason we get a rush from a big room, is that we think “Oh look, here are all these
people I can do business with.” However, that would all be based on follow up, wouldn’t it?
How good are you at following up with 200 people tomorrow? Do you have time for that?
Most professionals do not. Assess your commitment to this kind of strategy. 
Think about what networking really is. Isn’t it the opportunity to get to know, like and trust 
others? Isn’t it about serving and giving, as well as receiving? 
Reciprocal networking experiences can come about quickly once your commitment to a
group of people becomes evident. If you have the attitude that you are there to ‘take,” or
even to “try” it out, people sniff that out. Do you want to give your heart and soul to
someone who is there to “take” you? Or even “try” you? Of course not. 
Some people also go to networking meetings to meet the “right” people. They want only a 
certain level of income in the room, or status. Usually those that network this way are 
unsuccessful because the “right” people are seldom in any room stacked up to jump into
your opportunity or to buy your products and services. Instead look for people who know
other people. If you need to meet professionals, what industries work with those
professionals? Who has the same target market? Network with those who will become
trusted associates who can bring you the needle in their haystack, so you don’t have to meet
thousands of people. You just need a few good friends who bring you great referrals. 
Real networking is about building long-term, solid, and lasting business relationships. It is
more like looking for a family than a quick stop. People who care about you will bring you
their best client who is already a buyer of their services and a good customer who can be
counted on. 
Look for a group that meets weekly and invest some time in getting to know the other 
members. If you need clientele with 10 employees or more, spend time with the accountants, 
the group benefits providers, and the printers who have hundreds of these as clients. Win
the other networkers over and they will lead you to the masses you want to have as
customers. 
Nobody gives their clients to fly by night visitors on stop overs, they give their best
clients and best friends to friends they network with consistently.

2. BOOK A MEETING FROM THE MEETING 
You go out to a networking event, why are you there? Why is everyone in the room there?
Are we not all there for the purpose of networking? 
Whether we are at a live event or an online event, people are in the room for the purpose of 
networking if it is called a “Networking Event.” You are there, and they are there, so why
would you wait to call them tomorrow? What is the point of that? 
Everyone is busy tomorrow. Nobody is answering their phone. You are there now. They are 
there now. Pull out your calendar and book the appointment – NOW! 
Look for networking events that promote real networking because other kinds of events may 
give you valuable information, but if you need leads and referrals, those may not be the
events where you want to spend your time. In fact, you may be wasting a lot of time, which is
why networking is not working for you currently. 
If you are attending a live event that is called a “Networking Event,” and a speaker comes
out to sell you something for the whole event, that is not actually a networking event. It is a 
speaker or a salesperson using the word “Networking” to lure you in. 
If you are attending a “Networking” event online and you are not allowed to chat with the
other attendees in private messaging. This is not a “Networking” event. Someone is
controlling the contact between participants and that makes the event something else. There
is likely a speaker wanting to sell you something, so they don’t want you buying from others
in the room or being distracted from what they are selling. 
There is no problem with taking courses, if that is what you are there for. Courses are great. 
We recommend and take a lot of courses on many subjects, and we even sell some, but 
networking is networking. Networking exists so you as a business owner, professional, or 
entrepreneur can connect with others like yourself with the purpose of getting business, 
sharing warmed up leads, creating collaborative alliances, giving and receiving
recommendations and Google reviews, or just plain making money. Networking is about 
working your nets. It is a time to be about YOU and the other participants in the room. How
can you do that if the focus is all on the presenter with no time for actual networking? 
In a real networking event, whether it is live or online, you the networker can book a meeting
easily with another attendee. This makes it immediately beneficial for you to attend. Every
meeting is a potential sale or referral for a sale. How many of those meetings would you
like this week?

3. ASK FOR REFERRALS 
Want to get lots of referrals? The first and most important step to getting lots of referrals is
simple. Just ask. You will get zero if you don’t ask. You will get some if you ask. The more
you ask, the more you get. The more you work on the process of asking, the better your ask
will be, and the more referrals you will get. 
Have you been asking for referrals? Have you really been asking? What does that look like? 
Do you have a script where you ask the same way 20 times or 100 times, before you assess 
your data? When you are selling yourself, your product, or your service you need to look at
it like a science. If 6 people do not give you the right response, how would you know what
you did right or wrong? That test group is too small. Do everything you do, exactly the same
way, every time and count the responses, and write them down in a day timer or a journal so
you know your numbers. 
Create a script for asking referrals. Create a script for prospects and create a different script 
for clients. Use this script when you do one-on-one meetings with people and count your 
results with each of these scripts. Why have two scripts? 
Prospects do not know you. They do not love you. You can not ask them for as much. They 
are not committed to you yet. They also do not know your product or service so they do not 
trust you with as much. During a one-on-one start out by asking about them and then talk 
about you and your business. If they are not looking like they are going to become an
instant client, ask for a referral. Try something simple like “I know you have not used my
service yet, but based on what I just told you about what I do can you think of 3 people who
need my service?” Why ask for one when you can ask for three? If you ask for three you will 
consistently get more referrals. 
With clients ask for referrals right after the purchase has been made. Obviously they found 
value. Right now is the best time to ask. Ask at the till, or ask at the end of the meeting, or
go back to previous clients and book a one-on-one. Take them some added value, and ask
for 10 referrals. Why ask for one when you can ask for 10? If you doubled or tripled the
amount of referrals coming in for your business, what would that do for your cash flow?

4. ASK FOR THE SALE 
How many times have you assumed someone would not buy, so you did not ask for the
sale? 
Do not make up excuses for your prospect before they gave you an excuse of their own.
People can dream up their own excuses just fine, you do not need to let them off the hook
in your mind before you even ask. We do this all the time. We think to ourselves: 
They are too broke. 
This is too short of notice to ask. 
The stars are not aligned in the heavens for the perfect presentation. 
I need to get to know them better. 
We are just making up their excuses for them. Give them the opportunity to answer you 
themselves. Who knows, maybe they are interested in your offer. 
It is true that there are some occasions where you need to get to know someone before
you can talk about business, but when you have a networking meeting, and it leads to a
one-on-one, why do you think you have to wait to ask? 
Was this person met at a networking meeting? If they were, they were there to sell you
something. They are networking because they have a product or a service, just like you do. 
They want you to buy from them, or refer them to someone who will buy from them. 
Sometimes you may do so because it is something you needed, or you were going to buy it 
anyway. Sometimes you may give them a referral, or even three referrals if they ask you for 
them. They should expect to pitch you, and you should expect to pitch them. Are you not
in business? Are they not in business? 
If you do not ask for the sale, it is almost certain that you will not make a sale. It is a rare
client who begs you for the opportunity to buy without having been asked. If you do ask for
the sale 10 times, it is likely that with some practice you will make a sale at least 2 out of 10
times with most products and services. It is also likely that you would receive referrals each
time you do not get a sale, if you asked for them. Do some math. If you have 5 more
appointments each week online or live, and ask for a sale what will that do for your bottom
line? Do you need twice as much money? Then book 10 more appointments each week,
and ask for the sale every time you have a presentation of any kind. Sales is simple
math. Ask and do the math.

5. AUTOMATE FOLLOW UPS 
One of the biggest reasons that people do not make money from networking is that they
lack the proper follow up skills, or they do not have any processes set up to be sure that
follow up happens. 
Are you a busy person? Busy people need to automate as much of their processes as
possible so they can get things done. 
Use a good Customer Retention Management system or a CRM. You want something where 
you can take pictures of the business cards you collected and they will automatically go into 
your contact list. As you collect cards, ask people if you can send them information about
what you do. This is called an “Opt-In.” Once they say yes, and you have their business card,
you have permission to send them emails. Have an email explanation with an invitation to
meet for coffee automated, so as soon as you put the name into the contact list they get that
message to set up a meeting. 
Use an admin, a student, or your spouse to help you add new connections that you meet 
online or in live meetings to your LinkedIn connections and/or your Facebook friends list.
Every time they see you post, you are warming them up for future communications, and
potential sales. Since it takes time for you to often get people on the phone after an event,
this keeps them in the loop about who you are, and what you do. They will feel like they
know you before they get together with you again. Get the help you need to get this person
into your automation sequences and your social media connections as quickly as you meet
them. A steady flow of new connections, is a steady flow of new appointments, and
appointments lead to sales. 
It takes 7 to 14 touches to get a sale. This means it takes more than one conversation to win 
them over to becoming your client, or even giving you a referral. Social media posting,
emails, blogging, videos, texting, and smoke signals all count as touch points. Set as many of
these up in advance as possible and automate them. 
When following up by phone or electronic messaging, ask for an appointment. Sales get
made on appointments. Book the appointment for as quickly as possible. When someone
says they can not meet in person, book a meeting online. If they cannot meet this week,
book it for next week. If they cannot meet for a month, this information should go into your
CRM and it should be set up so you get an automated task reminder in 30 days to call them
for the appointment. 
Automate every part of the follow up process that you can. Automation is like electronic
elves that keep working night and day to bring you pay.
