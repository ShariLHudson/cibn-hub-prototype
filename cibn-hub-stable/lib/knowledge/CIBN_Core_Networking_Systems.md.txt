CIBN One-on-One Preparation System
KNOWLEDGE FILE ROUTING GUIDE
Use this knowledge file when users ask about:
• preparing for a one-on-one meeting
• preparing for networking conversations
• how to prepare before meeting someone
• questions to ask during networking meetings

This file explains the CIBN one-on-one preparation workflow and helps members prepare for meaningful networking conversations.
________________________________________
Purpose of One-on-One Conversations
One-on-one meetings are one of the most important activities within the CIBN networking system.
These conversations help members:
• understand each other's businesses
• identify shared audiences
• recognize complementary services
• explore potential collaboration opportunities
• discover referral pathways
The purpose of a one-on-one conversation is relationship discovery, not selling.
________________________________________
When to Use This Preparation System
Members may use this preparation process when they:
• schedule a one-on-one meeting
• are preparing for a networking conversation
• want to better understand someone they are meeting
• want to identify potential collaboration opportunities
________________________________________
Two-Step Preparation Workflow
The preparation system gathers two types of information before generating meeting guidance.
Step 1 — Information about the person being met
Step 2 — Information about the member’s own business
The assistant should pause after each step until information is provided.
Do not generate the preparation dashboard until both inputs are received.
________________________________________
Step 1 — Information About the Person You Are Meeting
Prompt the user to paste information about the person they will be meeting.
Example prompt:
Step 1 — Information About the Person You Are Meeting
Please paste any information you have about the person you will be meeting.
Helpful examples include:
• their LinkedIn profile
• their LinkedIn “About” section
• company website description
• services they provide
• notes from a previous conversation
A LinkedIn profile alone is often enough to begin.
Pause until the user provides this information.
________________________________________
Step 2 — Information About Your Business
After Step 1 is completed, request information about the member’s business.
Example prompt:
Step 2 — Information About Your Business
Now please paste a short description of your business so I can identify collaboration opportunities.
Helpful information includes:
• types of clients you serve
• industries you work with
• services you provide
• your ideal client
Tip for members:
Many professionals keep a short description of their business saved in a document so they can quickly paste it before meetings.
Pause until this information is provided.
________________________________________
Step 3 — Generate the One-on-One Preparation Dashboard
After both inputs are received, generate a Meeting Preparation Dashboard.
The dashboard helps the user enter the conversation with clarity.
________________________________________
One-on-One Preparation Dashboard
Meeting Overview
Provide a short summary of the person being met.
Include:
• industry
• services offered
• types of clients served
• professional focus
________________________________________
Collaboration Signals
Evaluate the potential relationship using three signals.
Shared Audience
Both professionals serve similar types of clients.
Example:
Both work with small business owners.
________________________________________
Complementary Services
Both professionals serve the same clients but offer different services.
Example:
A marketing consultant and a CPA.
________________________________________
Referral Pathways
One professional regularly encounters clients who may benefit from the other’s services.
Example:
A financial advisor whose clients frequently need legal services.
________________________________________
If multiple signals are present, highlight the possibility of a collaborating partner relationship.
________________________________________
Conversation Starters
Provide a few natural opening questions.
Examples:
• What types of clients are you hoping to meet more of this year?
• Where do most of your referrals come from today?
• What types of projects excite you most right now?
________________________________________
Strategic Discovery Questions
These questions help uncover collaboration opportunities.
Examples:
• Which professionals usually work with your clients before they meet you?
• What types of businesses benefit most from your services?
• Are there professionals you collaborate with regularly?
________________________________________
What to Listen For
Encourage the user to listen for signals such as:
• types of clients mentioned
• industries served
• professionals they frequently collaborate with
• referral sources they rely on
These clues can reveal opportunities for collaboration.
________________________________________
Tailored Introduction
Generate a brief introduction the member could use during the conversation.
Structure:
1.	Name
2.	Who they help
3.	The outcome they create
4.	Shared audience context
5.	Name again
Example structure:
Hi, my name is [Name].
I help [type of client] achieve [result].
Since you also work with [shared audience], I’m always interested in meeting professionals connected to that community.
If you happen to meet people looking to [result], those introductions are always appreciated.
Again, my name is [Name].
________________________________________
Possible Follow-Up
Suggest possible next steps after the meeting.
Examples include:
• scheduling another conversation
• introducing each other to relevant contacts
• sharing helpful resources
• exploring collaboration opportunities
________________________________________
Using CIBN Tools After the Conversation
The following tools may support ongoing networking activity.
________________________________________
LinkedIn
LinkedIn is typically where:
• connections are maintained
• conversations continue
• introductions are made
Members often connect with people after one-on-one meetings to continue the relationship.
________________________________________
CIBN Chrome Extension
The Chrome Extension can help members identify additional networking opportunities.
Match Me
Match Me helps identify people in your network who may match a particular audience or collaboration opportunity.
Members may use Match Me when:
• identifying potential collaborating partners
• searching for professionals connected to certain industries
• exploring referral opportunities
________________________________________
Nolodex
If a conversation leads to a referral opportunity, members may track it in Nolodex.
Nolodex may track:
• referrals given
• referrals received
• opportunities created through introductions
• referral fees or payments
Simple way to think about it:
LinkedIn → relationships and introductions
Nolodex → opportunity and referral tracking
________________________________________
Key Reminder
One-on-one conversations are about learning and relationship building.
When members take time to prepare for these conversations, they are more likely to:
• identify collaboration opportunities
• develop trusted relationships
• generate meaningful introductions
Over time, these relationships may develop into collaborating partners who regularly introduce opportunities.

# Core Concept of the CIBN Networking System

CIBN focuses on developing 3–5 strong collaborating partners.

Collaborating partners are professionals who:

• serve the same audience
• offer complementary services
• understand each other's ideal clients
• introduce opportunities when appropriate

Rather than trying to build hundreds of networking contacts, the CIBN approach focuses on building a small group of trusted professionals who regularly introduce opportunities.________________________________________
Purpose of the CIBN Networking System
CIBN is designed to help professionals move from random networking to intentional relationship building.
Instead of relying on large numbers of contacts, the CIBN approach focuses on developing 3–5 strong collaborating partners who introduce opportunities over time.
These relationships help create:
• trusted introductions
• collaboration opportunities
• warmed-up referrals
• consistent networking results
________________________________________
The CIBN Networking Workflow
The CIBN networking process generally follows this progression.
1.	Meet someone
2.	Schedule a one-on-one conversation
3.	Prepare for the conversation
4.	Identify collaboration signals
5.	Explore referral opportunities
6.	Make introductions
7.	Track opportunities

Referral conversations help identify:

________________________________________
Welcome to CIBN
Becoming a member of CIBN means joining a networking community focused on building trusted professional relationships that lead to collaboration and referrals.
The goal is not simply to meet many people, but to build a small group of strong collaborating partners who can introduce opportunities over time.
________________________________________
Step 1 — Schedule Your Orientation Call
New members are encouraged to begin with an orientation call.
The orientation helps members:
• understand how the CIBN networking system works
• learn how to use the available tools
• clarify their ideal clients
• begin identifying potential collaborating partners
You can schedule an orientation here:
https://cibnconnect.com/membership-options
Scroll to the bottom of the page to book time with Joshua.
________________________________________
Step 2 — Your First 48 Hours
During your first couple of days as a member, focus on a few simple actions.
Recommended first steps include:
• connect with members you meet through networking events
• review the CIBN networking workflow
• install the CIBN Chrome Extension if available
• begin identifying professionals who serve the same audience as you
These early steps help you begin building relationships within the community.
________________________________________
Step 3 — Begin One-on-One Conversations
One-on-one meetings are a key part of the CIBN networking process.
These conversations help members:
• understand each other’s businesses
• identify collaboration opportunities
• explore referral pathways
Members often schedule one-on-one conversations with professionals they meet through networking events.
________________________________________
Step 4 — Identify Collaborating Partners
One of the most important goals in the CIBN system is developing 3–5 strong collaborating partners.
Collaborating partners are professionals who:
• serve a similar audience
• offer complementary services
• understand each other's ideal clients
• are comfortable introducing opportunities
Rather than relying on large numbers of contacts, the CIBN approach focuses on building a small group of trusted professional relationships.
________________________________________
Step 5 — Participate in Networking Events
Networking events provide opportunities to:
• meet other professionals
• learn about different businesses
• build new relationships
• schedule one-on-one conversations
These events help members expand their professional network and discover collaboration opportunities.
________________________________________
Step 6 — Explore the CIBN Tools
Several tools support the CIBN networking system.
Members may use:
LinkedIn
to build and maintain professional relationships.
CIBN Chrome Extension
to identify networking opportunities within LinkedIn.
Meetn
to participate in networking meetings and webinars.
Nolodex
to track referrals and opportunities generated through networking.
Each tool supports a different part of the networking process.
________________________________________
Key Reminder for New Members
The most important goal during the early stages of membership is building relationships.
Many members begin by:
• meeting other professionals
• scheduling one-on-one conversations
• identifying shared audiences
• exploring collaboration opportunities
Over time, these relationships may develop into collaborating partners who introduce opportunities.
