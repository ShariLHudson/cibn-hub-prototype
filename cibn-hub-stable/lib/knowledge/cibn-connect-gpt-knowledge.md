The Networking Success Path (5 Stages) – What It Is and How to Use It
What it is:
• A roadmap for moving from a brand-new member to an influencer whose voice attracts opportunities and
consistent business.
• A tool to avoid common networking frustrations that come from misunderstanding what stage your
business is currently in.
How to use it (the exact decision flow from the transcript content you provided):
1 Ask yourself: “Where am I on the path right now?”
2 Ask yourself: “Where do I want to go next?”
3 Pick the stage that matches your current reality (partnerships, visibility, speaking, systems, scale).
4 Do the recommended activities for that stage to graduate to the next stage.
5 Repeat: this is a progressive ladder; each stage builds the foundation for the next.
Stage 1: Build
• Goal: Form real, trust-based business friendships.
• Create 25 new connections and find 5 collaborating partners.
• Work through the 7 Videos for Networking Success to master fundamentals.
• Prioritize meeting the right people and nurturing those relationships.
• Think bigger than sales one at a time: focus on partners who can introduce you to many prospects.
Stage 2: Socialize
• Goal: Expand your reach with online networking strategies.
• Refine marketing and online presence; create brand coherence and make it easy to share your
business.
• Complete the Social Media Bootcamp.
• Develop content strategies to stay visible online.
• Grow by 500 new contacts through consistent outreach.
• Create content promotion partnerships.
Stage 3: Present
• Goal: Grow confidence and master presentation skills by leveraging existing platforms.
• Take a presentation spot and a Summit spot inside CIBN Connect.
• Record a CIBN video and write an article for the website.
• Earn CIBN Connect Speaker Certification.
• Take speaking opportunities on digital media platforms.
Stage 4: Produce
• Goal: Systematize results and accelerate performance by creating your own platform.
• Focus on goal setting and measurable outcomes.
• Work in 90-day segments to track progress.
• Create affirmations tied to business goals.
• Create marketing tools (newsletters, articles, videos) and give them a home that is easy to find.
Stage 5: Influence
• Goal: Scale your impact and become an influencer in your space.
• Build toward at least 10,000 online connections.
• Automate your content strategy with blogs, podcasts, or vlogs.
• Empower your community to amplify your message.
• Collaborate with other high-quality platforms.
• Uplift the ones following in your steps.

How To: Set Up a Scoring Matrix (and Use Linked Analytics)
What the scoring matrix does:
• Assigns a score to each person you meet on LinkedIn based on your search parameters and goals.
• Helps you design intentional records and segment your networking toward the outcomes you want.
Step-by-step (as described):
1 Open the main home screen of the software.
2 Click the “two heads” icon to access scoring matrix setup (if not set up yet, it may appear flashing).
3 Set 3 categories of search parameters: (1) geographic positive, (2) geographic negative, and (3)
keywords/qualifiers.
4 Geographic positive: enter locations you want to score higher.
5 Geographic negative: enter locations you want to score lower (if you have service-area restrictions).
6 Keyword/qualifier tags: enter simple tags separated by commas (short phrases or simple sentences are
used in the example).
7 Make sure your keywords are likely to appear on a person’s LinkedIn profile (avoid invisible intent like
“wants to buy”).
8 Save and use the matrix; adjust later as your strategy changes (you can update the matrix anytime).
Two ways to think about your keyword strategy (from the video):
• Direct client targeting: type in your ideal client traits. This can work but tends to find leads one at a time.
• Referral partner targeting (long-term): type in traits of people who already have access to your ideal
clients. This can create more consistent referrals over time.
Example keyword tags used in the transcript (demonstration):
• Buying authority (business owners / CEOs).
• In business for more than 3 years (signals established business and likely budget).
• Sells products or services online.
• More than 1,000 LinkedIn connections (well-connected individuals).
How To: Use Best Practices While Networking With the Extension
Foundation rule:
• Add connections with the intention of building and growing the relationship for future opportunities.
• The software helps identify who your connections know, but the relationship quality determines
outcomes.
How to follow up after requesting an introduction (step-by-step):
1 Make the introduction request using the extension.
2 Send a direct message on LinkedIn to your connection (the potential introducer).
3 In your message, mention you made an introduction request and ask if they would mind following
through.
4 Explain the purpose: building the relationship so the software can help you leverage the connection to
meet more people in your target demographic.
5 Proceed once they are aware and ready; this reduces missed requests and improves follow-through.
How to be a high-quality connector after an introduction:
• Do not make an introduction and disappear.
• Follow up to see if they met, booked a call, or are working on something together.
• Treat follow-through as part of being a high-quality networker.
How to use introduction posts for engagement:
1 After creating an introduction, use the post the software creates for you.
2 Share the post on LinkedIn so others see you are a connector who delivers.
3 Use this as an authentic engagement driver that signals credibility and community participation.

How To: Use Additional Tools on a LinkedIn Profile Page
1) Profile score (and why someone got that score)
1 Visit a person’s LinkedIn profile.
2 Look for the score attached to their profile (added by the extension).
3 Hover over the score to see why the score was given and how it aligns to the goals you set in your
AI-guided goal metrics.
2) Golden recommendations
• Golden recommendations are a trust signal: consistent, reliable, and strong partnership.
• Example shown: a profile with many golden recommendations (used as a social proof trust indicator).
How to give a golden recommendation (step-by-step):
1 Open a trusted connection’s LinkedIn profile.
2 Use the Golden Recommendation tool in the extension.
3 Write the recommendation (the software can create an accompanying message).
4 Send the message to let them know you recommended them.
5 The recommendation appears as a visible trust marker (e.g., under a thumbs-up area).
3) Request an introduction from a mutual connection (Introduction button)
1 On the profile you want to meet, click the “Introduction” button in the extension.
2 Type the name of the mutual connection you want to introduce you.
3 Select the person when they appear.
4 Use the AI-guided system’s drafted introduction message: it provides profile summaries of both parties
and a draft message.
5 Tweak the draft slightly to make it personal and effective.
6 Send the request; the action is logged so you can track it later.
4) Track introductions and referrals (Full History)
1 Open the “Full History” section in the extension.
2 Review how many referrals and introductions you are getting.
3 Use this history to stay accountable and track outcomes over time.
5) Deep insights / personality summary
• Creates a condensed one-paragraph profile summary using information available on the LinkedIn page.
• Includes personality-style outputs based on profile information (as described in the transcript).
How to use deep insights (2 main use cases from the transcript):
1 Before or during networking meetings: use it as a quick reminder of who someone is and what they do.
2 Profile audit: review your own profile summary to compare the impression you intend versus what is
being received, then adjust your profile accordingly.
6) Trusted connection mapping / network view
• Shows how many connections someone has and how well-networked they are.
• Helps you see whether they network with the kinds of people you want to meet.
• Helps you trace pathways to the types of contacts you are seeking.
How To: Use the Match Button Function (Find High-Likelihood People to 
Meet + Request Warm Intros)
What Match Button does (from the transcript):
• Creates a shortlist of people you are likely to want to meet based on your scoring criteria and AI
guidance.
• Shows mutual connections so you can request warm introductions.
Step-by-step (as described):
1 Open Match Button and review the list of suggested people to meet (a shortlist is shown in the video).
2 Click a person from the list to investigate their profile.
3 Identify your mutual connection shown by the system.
4 Decide whether you truly want to meet the person (check the profile first).
5 Request an introduction: your mutual connection receives a pop-up that says you would like to meet the
person.
6 The mutual connection can approve (yes) or decline (no) and may provide a reason.
Important trust rule emphasized in the transcript:
• If you do not have enough trust with your mutual connection, you may not feel comfortable requesting
introductions.
• When trust is low or it has been a while: reach out, book a catch-up, rebuild familiarity and trust, then
request introductions.
Rebuilding trust before asking (the exact pattern described):
1 Message the person: “It’s been a while, why don’t we catch up?”
2 Get a meeting on the books and reconnect.
3 At the end of the meeting: ask if they would feel comfortable making a few introductions, since you
noticed they have high-quality connections.
4 Start small, and increase as trust and follow-through are established.
