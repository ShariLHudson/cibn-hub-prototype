# CIBN — What Is Networking Worth?
## Overview
This file covers the mindset, attitudes, and realistic expectations that determine whether someone succeeds or fails at networking. The AI uses this content to help users examine their beliefs about networking, understand what real value looks like, and build the right foundation before diving into tactics.

---

## Why Mindset Matters More Than Method
Networking results are shaped as much by what we believe as by what we do.

If we believe networking is fun and that business comes easily through it — we will have impressive experiences every time. If we believe networking is a waste of time and never leads to anything good — it won't.

The distance between those two outcomes is about 15 centimetres — the space between your ears.

Before focusing on tactics, ask honestly: **How do I feel about networking?**

Identifying that feeling is the first step to changing it.

---

## Common Misconceptions That Hold People Back
These beliefs keep business owners from succeeding at networking:

- "Networking only works in large groups"
- "The right person isn't in this room"
- "These people don't have the money to buy what I sell"
- "I have better things to do"
- "I'm no good at it"
- "This is too hard"

Each of these beliefs acts as a ceiling. Recognizing them is the first step to removing them.

## Does Group Size Matter?
Bigger is not better. Smaller groups consistently outperform large ones.

**Why small groups work better:**
- Members see each other week after week and build real relationships
- Know, like, and trust develops faster in a smaller setting
- People genuinely think about how to help each other
- Accountability is natural — not forced

**Real example:** A group of only eight members, meeting weekly, produced more revenue per member than any other club in the network. Results included:
- A print and promo business owner who had to hire new staff because his business grew too fast
- A life insurance agent reporting over $30,000 in commissions in a single quarter
- A realtor and mortgage broker consistently closing deals in a recessed market
- A network marketer who grew her business to the top of her industry and eventually retired to travel the world permanently

**Large networking events** tend to produce short-lived excitement and a pile of business cards nobody remembers the next morning. They feel productive but rarely create the deep relationships that generate consistent referrals.

If you can only connect meaningfully with 20 people per hour, why does it matter if there are 200 people in the room?

## "The Right Person Isn't in This Room"
This is one of the most common — and costly — beliefs in networking.

The people you are looking for are almost never physically in the room. But **everyone in the room knows hundreds of people they didn't bring with them.**

A professional networker doesn't look for their ideal client in the room. They look for people who have access to their ideal client — and build relationships with those people.

**Example:** If you sell to companies with 20–100 employees:
- The website designer in the room is already doing work for those companies
- The accountant in the room is already trusted by those owners
- The Tupperware seller in the room might be married to an oil and gas executive

Never disqualify anyone based on what they do for a living. You will never know who they know until you have the conversation.

---

## Giving Without Keeping Score
Some networkers track how much they give and create mental scorecards. This is the wrong approach entirely.

Giving is not giving if you are keeping score.

A referral is a gift — for three people:
1. **The member** who receives a warm lead
2. **The person being referred** who gains access to a trusted professional
3. **You, the giver** — who experiences the goodwill and reputation of being someone who connects people

Do not expect the person you referred to pay you back with a referral of equal value. Instead, expect your **networking organization** to serve you — through leads, advice, savings, skills, and relationships that compound over time.

## What Speeds Up Results
These qualities accelerate success in networking:

- Genuinely liking people
- Being willing to speak in front of groups (even imperfectly)
- Having a positive attitude toward learning
- Having basic sales skills and a follow-up process
- Connecting online after in-person events
- Booking a meeting from every meeting

---

## What Slows Down or Kills Results
These behaviors and beliefs create failure:

- Wrong expectations (expecting sales in the first 30 days)
- Believing you are better than others in the room
- Hating selling and refusing to improve
- Thinking you already know everything
- Avoiding public speaking rather than practicing it
- Not tracking activity or results

---

## Realistic Timelines
Networking is like planting a crop. There is a season for it.

- **Low-ticket products/services:** Results can come within weeks if members are local and incentivized
- **Mid-range offers ($500–$1,000):** Allow 2–3 months to begin seeing consistent leads
- **High-ticket offers ($1,000+):** May take 6–12 months to land the first sale — but when it comes, it is often accompanied by referrals and testimonials that accelerate future sales

**Nobody gives access to their best clients and closest friends to someone they just met.** Trust takes time. The methods in this system are designed to shorten that time — not eliminate it.

## The Real Value of a Business Network
Most people think of networking value only in terms of direct sales. The real value is much broader.

**Real examples of value beyond direct sales:**
- A realtor made no sales for three years — then closed nine deals in year four and grew consistently every year after
- She also eliminated her fear of public speaking, saved $2,000 on a roof repair through a member, improved her family's net worth by 40%, and lost 12 pounds working with a fitness coach in the group
- A single parent in direct sales invested a few hundred dollars in Bitcoin after learning about it from a member — and withdrew over $500,000 years later to buy a home
- A mortgage broker connected with a competitor in the network and used the conversation to build a multi-property rental portfolio — then passed the strategy to his children

**Other forms of real value:**
- Health coverage and life insurance guidance for self-employed business owners
- Travel insurance awareness (one hospital stay abroad without coverage = $100,000+)
- Protection from bad contractors, unfinished websites, and wasted marketing spend — because members are accountable to the group every week
- Access to trusted professionals at fair prices who do exceptional work because their reputation is on the line

---

## Accountability Changes Everything
One of the most overlooked benefits of a strong business network is accountability.

A website designer who takes $5,000 and disappears cannot hide in a group where they show up every week. A contractor who pockets a deposit and ghosts their client cannot face the room.

The best service many business owners ever receive comes through their network — because the people doing the work have something at stake beyond the transaction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long before I start seeing results from networking?**
A: It depends on your price point and industry. Lower-ticket offers may produce results within weeks. High-ticket services may take 6–12 months to land the first sale. The relationships you are building in the meantime have compounding value that often exceeds the direct sales.

**Q: Do I need a big group to get good leads?**
A: No. Data consistently shows that smaller groups — where people know, like, and trust each other — outperform large networking events. Relationships produce referrals. Numbers alone do not.

**Q: What if my ideal client isn't in the room?**
A: They don't need to be. Everyone in the room knows people they didn't bring. Your job is to build relationships with people who have access to the people you want to meet.

**Q: Should I track the referrals I give to make sure I'm getting back what I give?**
A: No. Giving with the expectation of equal return is not genuine giving — and people sense it. Trust your network to serve you over time. The returns come in many forms, not just direct referrals.

## Important Notes
- Mindset is not a soft concept — it directly determines networking outcomes
- Small, consistent groups outperform large events for long-term lead generation
- The value of a business network is much larger than direct sales alone
- Results require time, consistency, and a willingness to keep showing up
- Every person in the room knows someone you need to meet — never dismiss anyone
