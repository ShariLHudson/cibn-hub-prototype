# CIBN — New Member Onboarding

## Overview

This file guides new CIBN members through their first steps after joining. The AI uses this to help members understand what to do, where to start, and how to begin building relationships that lead to collaboration and referrals.

---

## Welcome to CIBN

Becoming a member of CIBN means joining a networking community focused on building trusted professional relationships.

👉 The goal is not to meet as many people as possible
👉 The goal is to build a small group of strong collaborating partners

These relationships lead to:

* Referrals
* Collaboration opportunities
* Long-term business growth

---

## Step 1 — Schedule Your Orientation

Start with an orientation call.

This helps you:

* Understand how the system works
* Learn how to use the tools
* Clarify your ideal clients
* Begin identifying collaborating partners

Schedule here:
https://cibnconnect.com/membership-options

👉 Scroll to the bottom of the page to book time

---

## Step 2 — Your First 48 Hours

Focus on a few simple actions:

* Connect with members you meet
* Review how the networking system works
* Install the Chrome Extension (if available)
* Identify professionals who serve the same audience as you

👉 These steps help you start building relationships immediately

---

## Step 3 — Begin One-on-One Conversations

One-on-one meetings are a key part of the CIBN system.

They help you:

* Understand other businesses
* Identify collaboration opportunities
* Explore referral pathways

👉 Most members start by scheduling conversations with people they meet at events

---

## Step 4 — Identify Collaborating Partners

Your goal is to develop **3–5 strong collaborating partners**

These are professionals who:

* Serve a similar audience
* Offer complementary services
* Understand your ideal clients
* Are willing to introduce opportunities

👉 Focus on quality relationships, not quantity

---

## Step 5 — Participate in Networking Events

Events help you:

* Meet professionals
* Learn about different businesses
* Build relationships
* Schedule one-on-one conversations

👉 This is where most relationships begin

---

## Step 6 — Use the CIBN Tools

Each tool supports a different part of the process:

* LinkedIn → build and maintain relationships
* Chrome Extension → identify opportunities
* Meetn → attend events and meetings
* Nolodex → track referrals and results

👉 You do not need to master everything immediately

---

## Suggested Next Steps

You may also want help with:

* Preparing for one-on-one conversations
* Understanding the networking workflow
* Building collaborating partner relationships

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I focus on first?**
A: Focus on meeting people and scheduling one-on-one conversations.

---

**Q: How many people should I connect with?**
A: Focus on building a few strong relationships instead of many loose ones.

---

**Q: Do I need to use all the tools right away?**
A: No. Start with relationships and conversations, then add tools over time.

---

## Important Notes

* Focus on relationships first
* Do not try to do everything at once
* Consistency matters more than speed
* Strong relationships lead to referrals over time
