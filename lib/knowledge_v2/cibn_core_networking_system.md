# CIBN — Core Networking System

## Overview

This file explains how the CIBN networking system works from start to finish. The AI uses this to guide users through how to build relationships, identify collaboration opportunities, make introductions, and track referrals using the CIBN approach.

---

## Core Concept of the CIBN System

CIBN focuses on developing **3–5 strong collaborating partners** instead of building a large network of loose connections.

Collaborating partners are professionals who:

* Serve the same audience
* Offer complementary services
* Understand each other’s ideal clients
* Introduce opportunities when appropriate

👉 The goal is **quality relationships, not quantity**

---

## Purpose of the CIBN Networking System

CIBN helps professionals move from:

* Random networking
  ➡️ to
* Intentional relationship building

This creates:

* Trusted introductions
* Collaboration opportunities
* Warm referrals
* Consistent business growth

---

## The CIBN Networking Workflow

### Step 1 — Meet Someone

Members meet professionals through:

* Networking events
* CIBN meetings
* Introductions
* LinkedIn

👉 Goal: Start a relationship (not sell)

---

### Step 2 — Schedule a One-on-One Conversation

Purpose:

* Understand each other’s business
* Identify shared audiences
* Explore collaboration

👉 Focus on discovery, not pitching

---

### Step 3 — Prepare for the Conversation

Members typically review:

* LinkedIn profile
* Website
* Services
* Client types

👉 Preparation increases the chance of meaningful conversations

---

### Step 4 — Identify Collaboration Signals

#### Shared Audience

Both serve similar clients

#### Complementary Services

Serve same clients but offer different services

#### Referral Pathways

One regularly meets clients who need the other

👉 Multiple signals = strong partnership potential

---

## Collaborating Partners

Collaborating partners:

* Serve a similar audience
* Offer complementary services
* Understand each other’s work
* Actively introduce opportunities

👉 CIBN encourages building **3–5 strong partners**

---

### Real-Life Example

A marketing consultant and a CPA:

* Serve small business owners
* Offer different services
* Can refer clients to each other

👉 This is a strong collaborating partner match

---

## Step 5 — Referral Conversations

These conversations focus on:

* Identifying introductions
* Exploring opportunities
* Understanding networks

👉 The goal is mutual value, not selling

---

## Step 6 — Making Introductions

Introductions often happen through LinkedIn.

**Example:**
“I thought the two of you might enjoy connecting. You both work with small business owners and may find it helpful to know each other.”

---

## Step 7 — Tracking Opportunities

### LinkedIn

Used for:

* Relationships
* Conversations
* Introductions

---

### Nolodex

Used for:

* Tracking referrals
* Recording opportunities
* Tracking referral payments

👉 Simple way to think about it:

* LinkedIn → relationships
* Nolodex → opportunity tracking

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many people should I network with?**
A: Focus on building 3–5 strong collaborating partners instead of trying to meet hundreds of people.

---

**Q: What makes someone a collaborating partner?**
A: They serve a similar audience, offer complementary services, and are willing to introduce opportunities.

---

**Q: Is networking about selling?**
A: No. The focus is relationship building, which leads to referrals over time.

---

## Important Notes

* Do not focus on volume of contacts
* Focus on depth of relationships
* Collaboration signals are key
* Relationships lead to referrals over time
