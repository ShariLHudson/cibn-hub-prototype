# CIBN — Infomercial Framework
## Overview
This file helps users craft short, effective business introductions for networking meetings. The AI uses this content to coach users on building infomercials that generate referrals and convert leads — based on Kerry George's proven CIBN framework.

---

## Why This Matters
An infomercial is not a sales pitch. It is a clarity tool — a short, scripted introduction designed to help others understand who you help and how, so they can refer you with confidence.

The most common mistake is standing up at a networking meeting and saying whatever comes to mind. That approach is as ineffective as traditional networking. A scripted, strategic infomercial is what creates results.

👉 If people don't understand what you do, they can't refer you.

---

## The One-Thing Rule
Always have a single ask in every infomercial.

If you ask for two things, listeners will do nothing. By the time you finish describing the second thing, they have forgotten the first.

- If you want an appointment booked — ask for that.
- If you want YouTube subscribers — ask for that.
- If you have two businesses, save one for another meeting.

**One infomercial. One ask. Every time.**

---

## Know Your Ideal Client
Before scripting your infomercial, get clear on your target market.

Ask yourself:
- Who are your current best clients?
- What do they have in common?
- Who do you most enjoy doing business with?
- Who would you like more of?

Choose a target group based on shared experience — not just a desire to help. Empathy comes from shared experience, not pity. Also make sure your target market can actually purchase your product or service.

---

## Script It and Stick to It
You cannot measure what you don't repeat.

Create a script and use the same infomercial at least 20 times before evaluating results. The first several deliveries are practice. You will not know if your infomercial is working if you change it every time.

**Make a plan. Work the plan. Never stray from the plan.**

## The Five Types of Infomercials

### #1 — The Basic Infomercial
The simplest format. State your name, your company, what you do, and make your ask.

**Best for:** New networkers who are still building confidence in front of a group.

**Example:**
> "Hello everyone. My name is Christine Block, and I am a bookkeeper. If you are currently trying to do the books for your business by yourself, you might want to hire me or refer me to a friend who is behind on their bookkeeping. Please book an appointment with me."

**Note:** This format is easy but tends to get lost in the "sea of sameness." Use it to get started, then graduate to a more effective method.

---

### #2 — The Testimony Infomercial
Share a real story a client told about you. Start with the story — not your name or services. If time allows, add those at the end.

**Why it works:** Stories are more interesting than facts. Listeners often report that hearing a testimony helps them immediately think of someone to refer.

**Example:**
> "Last week one of my clients said, 'Before you came along, I had all my receipts in a box and I could barely sleep. You've taken a great burden off me.' My name is Christine Block, and I help business owners get their receipts out of a box and into QuickBooks so they can relax and focus on their work. Who do you know that should book an appointment with me?"

**Tips:**
- Protect client confidentiality — you don't need to use their name.
- In online meetings, post your calendar link in the chat immediately after speaking.
- Post full contact info later in the meeting — keep the focus on your single ask right after your infomercial.

---

### #3 — The Results-Based Infomercial
Tell the story of a specific result you achieved for a client. Focus on the problem they had and confirm it was solved — you don't need to explain how.

**Why it works:** Reporting results positions you as an expert. It attracts people who want to do business with someone who clearly knows what they are doing.

**Example:**
> "About two months ago, one of my clients was contacted by the government for an audit. I contacted the department on her behalf and brought a quick resolution to their questions. She avoided the audit entirely. Do you know somebody who is concerned about getting audited? I would like to help them. My name is Christine, and here is my calendar link."

**Key question to close with:** "Who do you know that has this problem?" — This triggers a specific memory in your listeners.

---

### #4 — The Personality Style Infomercial
This advanced method speaks to all four personality types at once, so every person in the room feels like you are speaking directly to them.

#### The Four Personality Types

**Sanguine — "Is this fun?"**
Outgoing, energetic, loves people and a good time. If your infomercial is boring, they check out immediately. Use words like fun, exciting, and enjoyable.

**Choleric — "What are the fast facts?"**
Hard-driving, results-focused, impatient. They want the bottom line in three sentences or less. Use words like efficiency, profitability, and "I get things done."

**Analytical (Melancholy) — "Do you have all the details?"**
Thorough, organized, detail-oriented. They will read your entire website — twice. Tell them you have a plan, are well-organized, and have all the information they need.

**Phlegmatic — "Is this good for my family?"**
Easy-going, family-oriented, values rest and stability. They blend in and may be hard to read, but their top priority is family. Use words and themes that reflect care, stability, and family wellbeing.

#### Personality Style Example (Christine the bookkeeper):
> "Do you find bookkeeping boring? I love numbers and I am a fun bookkeeper who will make your record keeping process easy and stress free — like superwoman with a calculator! I swoop in and get things done and look after the details so you can focus on your business. Let me help you create more profit so you can give your family the time they deserve. If you want to be highly effective and organized in your bookkeeping without doing all the work yourself, I am your bookkeeper! My name is Christine Block. Here is my calendar link."

**How to apply:** Think about what you can say that shows:
- You are fun to work with (Sanguine)
- You get things done quickly (Choleric)
- You have all the details covered (Analytical)
- You care about what matters to their family or wellbeing (Phlegmatic)

---

### #5 — The Hybrid Infomercial
The most powerful format. Combines personality-based hot buttons with provocative opening questions to wake up a room full of listeners who have been hearing the same introduction over and over.

**Structure:**
1. Open with 2–3 strong, provocative questions (no name, no services yet)
2. Describe the results you create
3. Close with name and a single ask

**Why it works:** Everyone else opened with their name and a list of services. You open with a question that makes people think — and remember you.

**Example:**
> "Do you know somebody who keeps all their receipts in a box and loses sleep for three weeks at tax time? Have you ever met anyone who doesn't understand their balance sheet or what their expenses are each month? Do you know anyone who is going through an audit or afraid of one? I help busy business owners get their bookkeeping done on time so they have peace of mind — and the freedom to be the creative, fun, family-loving people they were meant to be. My name is Christine, and here is my link to book an appointment."

## Choosing Your Method

| Method | Best Use | Skill Level |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | Getting started, building confidence | Beginner |
| Testimony | Relatable stories, trigger referrals | Intermediate |
| Results-Based | Establish expertise, specific outcomes | Intermediate |
| Personality Style | Emotionally connect with entire room | Advanced |
| Hybrid | Maximum impact, wake up tired audiences | Advanced |

Start with the method that fits your current comfort level. Practice until it is natural, then level up.

## When to Use Your Infomercial
- One-on-one conversations
- Networking meeting introductions
- Online meetings (use chat for calendar link)
- Follow-up conversations
- Any event where you have 60 seconds or less

---

## Common Mistakes
- Saying whatever comes to mind — no script, no data, no improvement
- Asking for two things at once
- Starting with your name and a list of services (forgettable)
- Changing your infomercial every time (you can't measure what you don't repeat)
- Going over your allotted time (disrespectful to every other speaker)
- Picking a target market based on feelings rather than ability to buy

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long should my infomercial be?**
A: Sixty seconds or less. Respect the timekeeper at every event — going over time is disrespectful to other attendees.

**Q: Should I include everything I do?**
A: No. One message, one ask. Focus on the single most valuable result you create.

**Q: How many times should I use the same infomercial?**
A: At least 20 times before you evaluate whether it's working. The first several are practice.

**Q: Can I use client names in my infomercial?**
A: Only with permission. When in doubt, say "I had a client who..." to protect confidentiality.

**Q: What if I have two businesses?**
A: Save one for another meeting. Half of nothing is still nothing — do not split your 60 seconds.

## Important Notes
- A script is not optional — it is how you measure and improve results
- Clarity and specificity outperform creativity every time
- Focus on outcomes and results, not services
- The goal is to be referred — and people refer what they understand
- Your infomercial will evolve as you better understand your ideal client
